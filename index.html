Independent · Interactive Sim
Independent Project · 2026

Your Support Queue Is Fine — Until It Isn't

A discrete-event simulation of a multi-tier service queue, built to find the utilisation threshold where SLA compliance collapses. Models non-stationary Poisson arrivals, lognormal service times, four severity tiers, and four scheduling policies.

Shipped twice: a SimPy engine in Python for the analysis, and a browser dashboard that re-implements it in JavaScript — so anyone can move the sliders, watch the queue fill in real time, and re-run every experiment themselves. Including the Erlang-C validation suite: you can verify my numbers without taking my word for it.

The finding: priority queuing protects critical tickets by quietly destroying low-priority ones. At 95% utilisation, S1 waits stay flat at ~0.14h while S4's p95 reaches 6.3 hours — and nobody notices, because the 8-hour SLA window is wide enough that the dashboard still reads 97% green. EDF matches S1 protection while being measurably fairer to S4.

Engine validated against the Erlang-C closed form before any conclusion was drawn — 23 of 24 checks inside the 95% CI. Parameters are synthetic and stress-tested at ±30%, so the conclusions rest on what survives the range, not on any single number.

Capstone · ML
B.E. Capstone · 2022 – 2024

Predicting Mental-Health Treatment Seeking in Tech

A classifier trained on 1,259 survey responses (26 features) to predict whether an individual in the tech industry would seek mental-health treatment — the aim being earlier identification of people who need support but don't reach for it. Compared SVM, Random Forest, and Decision Tree, then a stacked ensemble. Deployed behind a Flask app.

The honest result: the stacked ensemble (0.83) did not beat the simplest strong baseline — a plain SVM at 0.85. Added complexity bought nothing. Worth reporting rather than burying: it's the more useful lesson.
